# Sprint Brief — Product v1

## 1. Scope Summary

Sprint v1 defines the Product baseline for `ticket_mafia`, a Vietnamese demo product for online football ticket sales across iOS, Android, Web, and an Admin Web portal.

The product lets customers log in with email or phone OTP, browse matches on sale, select concrete seat codes generated by section and floor, hold up to 5 seats for 10 minutes, view a configured transfer QR, submit payment completion, receive admin-confirmed QR/e-tickets, view purchase history, and exchange seats to equal-or-higher priced seats.

Admins manage matches, seat inventory, price versions, default transfer QR configuration, and pending payment confirmations. Gate scanning is represented as an API-driven state update that marks an e-ticket as scanned/used once.

## 2. Sprint Rationale

The current buying model is on-site purchase at the stadium. The demo goal is to show a complete online purchase and admin confirmation workflow without integrating a real payment gateway, queueing layer, or production-scale operational controls.

## 3. Review Notes

- Product is intentionally scoped as a demo; peak-load, queue/waiting-room, and production NFR targets are out of scope for v1 and captured as open risks.
- Payment is manual bank transfer via a configured default QR, not gateway reconciliation.
- Seat, order, ticket, and exchange lifecycles are product-critical and captured as business rules in `prd-v1.md`.
